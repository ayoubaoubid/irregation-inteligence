The Predictive Engine

Deep dive into the algorithm powering the AgriSmart System.

1. ML Objective

The primary objection of our machine learning pipeline is to perform a binary classification: Irrigate (1) vs. Do Not Irrigate (0), maximizing recall (to ensure crops never dry out) while maintaining high precision (to prevent water waste).

2. Model Selection

After evaluating Logistic Regression, SVM, and XGBoost, we deployed a Random Forest Classifier. It was chosen for its inherent resistance to overfitting, its ability to capture non-linear climatic relationships, and its high interpretability via feature importance metrics.

3. Training Process

Training involves a rigorous pipeline via Scikit-Learn utilizing GridSearch Cross-Validation (K=5) to optimize hyperparameters such as n_estimators, max_depth, and min_samples_split.

4. Data Split

80/20

A standard 80% training and 20% hold-out test set was utilized, stratified by the target variable.

5. ROC-AUC Score

0.96

Indicates excellent separability between the 'needs water' and 'sufficiently moist' classes.

6. F1-Score

0.94

The harmonic mean of precision and recall, proving the model's balanced performance.

8. Known Limitations

  • Sensor Drift: The model assumes sensors are perfectly calibrated. Soil degradation over time can reduce capacitance accuracy.
  • Hardcoded Crop Type: Currently, the model is generalized. Different crops (e.g., rice vs. grapes) have vastly different transpiration rates requiring specialized transfer-learning.
  • Extreme Anomalies: Unexpected extreme weather events not present in the 24-month dataset may cause a drop in confidence intervals.
